\section{Conclusion}

In this work, we presented a comparative study of several representative models, 
including convolutional, recurrent, transformer-based, and generative approaches. 
Through both quantitative and qualitative evaluations, we showed that diffusion-based 
methods achieve the strongest performance, while other architectures provide 
different trade-offs between efficiency and accuracy.
%
Our analysis highlights the importance of considering both performance and 
computational cost when selecting models for practical applications. Future work 
can explore more efficient variants of generative models, as well as evaluation 
on more diverse datasets and tasks to better understand their generalization 
capabilities.
